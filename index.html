Great Mosque of Mecca

The Great Mosque of Mecca is the largest mosque in the world and the holiest site in Islam. Every year, millions of Muslims from all over the world flock to Mecca to perform the Hajj pilgrimage.

The Great Mosque is built around the Kaaba, the most sacred spot in Islam. The Kaaba is a cuboid-shaped structure that is covered in a black cloth and is surrounded by a circular walkway. pilgrims circumnavigate the Kaaba seven times counter-clockwise as a symbolic act of worship.

The Great Mosque of Mecca is an awe-inspiring sight and a truly unique place of worship. If you are lucky enough to visit, be sure to dress modestly and respect the religious customs of those around you.

The Floating Mosque in Jeddah

The Floating Mosque in Jeddah is one of the most unique and beautiful mosques in the world. The mosque was built in the early 1980s and is located on a man-made island in the middle of a freshwater lake. The mosque is open to the public and visitors can take tours of the facility.

The capital city of Riyadh

Riyadh, the capital city of Saudi Arabia, is a fascinating place to explore. With its rich history and culture, there is much to see and do in this bustling city. From its stunning mosques and palaces to its traditional markets and vibrant nightlife, Riyadh has something to offer everyone.

If you're looking for a taste of the traditional Saudi way of life, be sure to visit the Old City. Here you'll find narrow streets lined with shops and stalls selling everything from spices to souvenirs. You can also haggle with the shopkeepers for a better price – it's all part of the fun!

For something a little different, head to Al Masmak Fort. This historic fort was once the home of the Saudi royal family, and today it houses a museum which tells the story of the Kingdom's history.
